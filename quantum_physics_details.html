Quantum Physics Simulation

May 2024 - September 2024
MATLAB Fortran Quantum Physics Simulation

Project Overview

This project involved developing computational models to simulate quantum phenomena, with a specific focus on quantum tunneling effects in semiconductor materials. The research aimed to better understand how quantum tunneling affects electron behavior in nanoscale semiconductor devices, which is crucial for advancing semiconductor technology.

Technical Approach

The simulation was developed using a multi-faceted approach:

  • MATLAB for initial algorithm development and data visualization
  • Fortran for high-performance computational routines
  • Implemented time-dependent Schr√∂dinger equation solvers
  • Created boundary conditions specific to semiconductor interfaces
  • Developed visualization tools to represent quantum probability densities

Key Findings

The simulation yielded several important insights:

  • Quantified the relationship between barrier width and tunneling probability
  • Identified non-linear effects in multi-barrier structures
  • Demonstrated how temperature affects tunneling behavior
  • Established correlations between theoretical predictions and experimental data

  • Providing educational tools for quantum physics visualization
